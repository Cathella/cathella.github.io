Overview:

Schoolist is a comprehensive web and mobile application that revolutionizes the school shopping experience for busy parents. With its seamless interface and convenient features, Schoolist simplifies the process of finding and purchasing school supplies for children. By offering a wide range of products, intuitive navigation, real-time order tracking, and secure payment integration, Schoolist ensures that parents can effortlessly meet their children's educational needs while saving valuable time and minimizing stress. This case study explores the UI/UX design process behind Schoolist, focusing on how the app's features and intuitive interface enhance the overall user experience for busy parents.

What is Schoolist Solving?

Busy parents face numerous challenges when it comes to school shopping for their children. The process of finding and purchasing school supplies can be time-consuming, overwhelming, and often involves visiting multiple stores or dealing with cumbersome online shopping platforms. Additionally, the lack of centralized information and limited visibility into product availability and pricing can lead to inefficiencies and frustration for parents.

Schoolist aims to solve these problems by providing a seamless and convenient solution for busy parents to shop for school requirements. By streamlining the entire school shopping experience, Schoolist saves parents valuable time and effort, enabling them to focus on other responsibilities while ensuring their children have all the necessary supplies for a successful academic year.

Defining the Problem

How will Schoolist address the challenges faced by busy parents during school shopping, including time-consuming, scattered shopping experiences and a lack of centralized information?

Goals

BUSINESS GOALS
User Acquisition and Retention:
Attract a significant user base of busy parents looking for a convenient school supply shopping solution.
Retain users by providing an excellent user experience, reliability, and ongoing value.

Market Expansion:
Expand the app's reach to cover a wide range of geographical locations and educational institutions to serve a broader audience.

Product Diversification:
Offer a wide selection of school supplies to cater to various grade levels, preferences, and requirements.

USER GOALS
Efficient School Supply Shopping:
Parents should be able to quickly and easily find and purchase all the necessary school supplies for their children, saving them time and effort.

Simplified Product Discovery:
Users should have access to a well-organized catalog of school supplies, making it simple to browse and discover the products they need.

Convenient Ordering Process:
The app should offer a seamless and user-friendly ordering process, from selecting items to making secure payments.

Secure Payment Options:
Ensure that payment methods are secure and user-friendly to build trust and protect user data.

Cost Savings:
Parents should have access to promotions, discounts, and special offers, helping them save money on school supplies.

Final Designs

Colors

1. Get-Started Screen

  • The onboarding journey begins with a welcoming "Get Started" screen.
  • This screen features a visually appealing background, and a brief tagline that highlights the app's value proposition.
  • It has a prominent "Get Started" button to encourage users to proceed.

2. Sign Up Screen

  • When users tap "Get Started" button on the Get-Started screen, they are directed to the Sign Up screen.
  • Here, users are prompted to enter their phone number, which serves as their primary identifier for the app.
  • There is a "Send Verification Code" button to initiate the phone number verification process.

3. Code Entry Screen (Verification)

  • After entering the phone number, users are taken to the Code Entry screen.
  • A verification code is sent to the provided phone number via SMS.
  • The screen has an input field for users to enter the received code.

4. Login Screen

  • Users who have already registered can skip the sign-up process and go directly to the Login screen.
  • Here, they have the option to log in with their phone number (and verification code) if they have previously set up an account.
  • There is a "Send Verification Code" button to initiate the phone number verification process.

5. Complete Profile Screen

  • For new users or those who haven't yet completed their profiles, the app will guide them to the "Complete Profile" screen.
  • Users are asked to provide additional information, such as their full name and location.
  • This information will be used for personalized recommendations and delivery purposes.

6. Dashboard Empty

  • When a user logs in, they are initially directed to the dashboard or home page.
  • Since it's empty for new users or when no shopping lists are created, the primary focus is on creating a new shopping list.
  • The dashboard includes a form with an input field to give the list a name (e.g., "Ella's Shopping List").

7. Shopping List Created

  • Shopping list successfully created. This screen show a button to add items to the list. A user is allowed to see clearly what to do next by adding this call to action.
  • Users are also able to see a summarized number of items in the shopping lists.

8. Shopping Screen Categories

  • After naming the shopping list, users are directed to the "Shopping" screen when they click the "Add Items" button.
  • The screen starts with categories of school supplies to choose from (e.g., "Notebooks", "Pencils", "School bags", etc.).
  • Users can select a category of items they want to add to their shopping list.
  • Each category has a title, a descriptive icon, and a total number of items in that category.
